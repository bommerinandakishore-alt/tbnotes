01

Definition

IRIS is a paradoxical clinical deterioration arising from an exaggerated or dysregulated inflammatory response following initiation of ART, directed against viable or non-viable antigens of opportunistic infections (or autoantigens), in the setting of immune reconstitution.

Clinical Definition — Dhasmana et al., Drugs 2008 "Worsening of signs and symptoms due to known infections, or the development of disease due to occult infections that results from an inflammatory response by a re-invigorated immune system following the initiation of antiretroviral therapy."
Programme Definition — NTEP / NACO (Working Definition) "The worsening of signs and symptoms due to known infections, or the development of disease due to occult infections within 6 weeks to 6 months after initiating ART, with an increase in CD4 count."
02

Epidemiology

  • Incidence: 10–30% of all patients initiating ART
  • Up to 25% in patients with CD4 <50 cells/mm³ at ART initiation
  • TB-IRIS accounts for ~30% of all IRIS events globally
  • TB-IRIS reported in 7–43% of HIV-TB coinfected patients starting ART
  • Occurs typically within 2–12 weeks of ART; late IRIS up to 6 months
  • Mortality is generally low, but CNS IRIS (TB meningitis, Cryptococcal meningitis) carries significant morbidity and mortality
  • India bears ~9% of global HIV-associated TB burden; HIV-TB death rate 15–18% under RNTCP
03

Pathogenesis

ART causes rapid HIV viral load suppression → dramatic CD4+ T-cell recovery → reconstituted immune system mounts a vigorous inflammatory response against residual microbial antigens (dead or dying organisms) that persisted during immunosuppression.

🔄
Th1 Restoration
Sustained IFN-γ, TNF-α, IL-2 response against MTB antigens after immune recovery
⚡
Cytokine Surge
TNF-α surge, IL-6 elevation, reduced Treg function → uncontrolled inflammation
🧲
T-Cell Migration
Massive trafficking of pathogen-specific T cells to sites of infection and inflammation
🦠
Antigen Burden
High viable/non-viable antigen load at ART initiation drives exaggerated response
💥
DTH Restoration
Delayed-type hypersensitivity recovery → exacerbates granulomatous inflammation
🔑
CCR5 / CXCR4 Upregulation
TB-induced chemokine receptor upregulation increases HIV replication locally, worsening immune dysregulation

Diagnosis & INSHI 2008 Criteria

IRIS is a diagnosis of exclusion. The International Network for the Study of HIV-associated IRIS (INSHI, 2008) requires ALL of the following:

Must be Present

  • Temporal association: new event within 3 months (typically 2–12 wks) of ART initiation
  • Evidence of immune reconstitution: CD4 rising AND/OR viral load falling
  • Clinical features consistent with inflammatory / immune reaction
  • Response consistent with OI-specific IRIS pattern

Must be Excluded

  • New opportunistic infection (not related to pre-existing OI)
  • ART treatment failure (viral load should be falling)
  • Drug toxicity / ARV or ATT side effects
  • Expected natural disease progression
  • Non-adherence to OI therapy

Key Investigations:

  • CD4 count + HIV viral load — confirm immune reconstitution (CD4 ↑, VL ↓)
  • Blood cultures, CSF analysis, culture of body fluids — to rule out new/treatment-resistant OI
  • Imaging (CXR, CT thorax/abdomen/brain) — assess new or worsening lesions
  • FNA / Histopathology of enlarged lymph nodes — granulomatous inflammation without viable organisms; necrosis
  • CBNAAT / Xpert MTB/RIF on sputum or tissue — confirms TB, detects rifampicin resistance
  • Serum CrAg (cryptococcal antigen) — before ART initiation in patients with CD4 <100 to screen for subclinical cryptococcosis
  • Inflammatory markers — CRP, ESR, ferritin, LDH (elevated, non-specific)

🟢 Mild IRIS
  • Localised symptoms, manageable fever
  • No organ dysfunction
  • NSAIDs: Ibuprofen 400 mg TDS
  • Continue ART + ATT unchanged
  • Reassurance; observation
🟡 Moderate IRIS
  • Worsening features, significant fever
  • Suppurating lymph nodes, moderate respiratory compromise
  • Prednisolone: 1.5 mg/kg/day × 2 weeks, then 0.75 mg/kg/day × 2 weeks, then taper
  • Surgical/needle drainage of abscesses
  • Continue ART
🔴 Severe / Life-threatening IRIS
  • CNS IRIS (TB/Cryptococcal meningitis), respiratory/renal failure
  • High-dose IV corticosteroids
  • ICU-level supportive care
  • Lumbar puncture + CSF drainage for raised ICP
  • Temporary ART cessation: ONLY if no other option
  • Thalidomide: considered in refractory TB-IRIS (off-label)
Key Principle IRIS is generally self-limiting. ART interruption is rarely necessary and carries risk of HIV disease progression, viral rebound, and CD4 decline. The decision to interrupt must be weighed carefully against the risk of life-threatening complications.
10

Prevention

  • Earlier HIV diagnosis — initiate ART before CD4 falls below 200 cells/mm³
  • Intensified case finding (ICF) — screen for TB, Cryptococcus, CMV before starting ART using 4-symptom complex (cough, fever, weight loss, night sweats)
  • Adequate OI treatment duration — optimal treatment of OI for minimum recommended duration before ART start
  • Optimal ART timing: CD4 <50 → within 2 weeks; CD4 ≥50 → within 2–8 weeks; CNS TB → delay ART 8 weeks (to reduce CNS IRIS risk)
  • Prophylactic corticosteroids — In high-risk patients (CD4 <100, ART within 30 days of ATT): Prednisone 40 mg/day × 2 weeks then 20 mg/day × 2 weeks reduces IRIS hospitalisations [Meintjes et al., NEJM 2010]
  • Serum CrAg screening — screen and treat subclinical cryptococcal disease before ART initiation in patients with CD4 <100 cells/mm³
  • BCG vaccine — withhold from symptomatic HIV-infected children (WHO recommendation)

Landmark Studies

SAPIT Trial
NEJM 2010 | South Africa
HIV-TB co-infected adults (n=642)
Early integrated ART (within 4 weeks of ATT) significantly reduced mortality vs. sequential therapy. IRIS was more frequent but manageable. Established ART must accompany TB treatment.
CAMELIA Trial
NEJM 2011 | Cambodia
HIV-TB (n=661), CD4 median 25 cells/mm³
ART at 2 weeks vs 8 weeks after ATT: 2-week arm had significantly lower mortality (HR 0.62, p=0.006). IRIS more common in 2-week arm. Established early ART reduces AIDS/death in severely immunosuppressed patients.
Meintjes et al.
NEJM 2010 | South Africa
TB-IRIS, randomised (n=110)
Prednisolone 1.5 mg/kg × 2 wks then 0.75 mg/kg × 2 wks significantly reduced days hospitalised, therapeutic procedures, and IRIS severity vs. placebo. Established steroid regimen for moderate-severe TB-IRIS.
COAT Trial
Lancet ID 2014 | Uganda/S.Africa
Cryptococcal meningitis + HIV (n=177)
Deferring ART 5 weeks after antifungal therapy reduced Cryptococcal IRIS and mortality compared to early ART (at 1–2 weeks). Established the practice of deferring ART in cryptococcal meningitis.
